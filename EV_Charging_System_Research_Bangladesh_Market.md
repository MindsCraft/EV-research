### Prepared for Board Review | June 2026

## Executive Summary

Bangladesh is entering a pivotal window for EV charging infrastructure. The convergence of **zero import duties** (FY 2026-27), **rapid Chinese EV adoption**, a **government target of 1,200 charging stations by 2026**, and a market with only 2 active operators creates a rare first-mover opportunity.

This briefing covers the current market landscape, the technology landscape, sourcing strategy from China, business model options, and the financial case for entry.

4. **Market is wide open** — only 2 operators currently active nationwide
5. **Policy momentum** — *Electric Vehicle Industry Development Policy 2025* formalises CPO registration, tariffs, and investment incentives

## 🚗 2. Chinese EVs Entering Bangladesh — Connector Standards

Understanding which vehicles are coming directly determines which charger types we must supply.

| Brand | Models in BD | Local Importer | AC Connector | DC Connector |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **BYD** | Atto 3, Sealion 5/6 | CG-Runner Bangladesh | **Type 2** | **CCS2** |
| **Changan / Deepal** | Deepal S07, L07 | DHS Autos | **Type 2** | **CCS2** |
| **Chery** | Tiggo EV series | Asian MotorspeX | **Type 2** | **CCS2** |
| **GAC** | AION series | Local dealers | **Type 2** | **CCS2** |
| **MG (SAIC)** | MG4, MG5 | Rangs Motors | **Type 2** | **CCS2** |

> **Key Finding:** All Chinese EVs sold in Bangladesh use the international export specification — **Type 2 (AC charging) and CCS2 (DC fast charging)**. This is our target standard.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> China's domestic charging standard (GB/T) is **incompatible** with vehicles sold in Bangladesh. All imports must be **Type 2 / CCS2** specification — not GB/T.

**Why this category first:**

- Chinese EVs sold in Bangladesh ship with basic slow-charge cables only — owners immediately seek an upgrade

## 🏗️ 6. Technology Stack — How a Charging Network Works

Understanding the full technology stack is essential for evaluating partners and build-vs-buy decisions.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│         Customer-Facing App         │  ← Mobile app / RFID card / QR code
└──────────────────┬──────────────────┘
                   │
┌──────────────────▼──────────────────┐
│   Charging Management System (CMS)  │  ← Backend platform: billing, monitoring,
│         / OCPP Backend              │    user management, analytics
└──────────────────┬──────────────────┘
                   │ OCPP 1.6J / 2.0.1 protocol
┌──────────────────▼──────────────────┐
│     EV Charger Hardware (EVSE)      │  ← The physical units we import
└──────────────────┬──────────────────┘
                   │
┌──────────────────▼──────────────────┐
│         EV Vehicle Battery          │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**OCPP** (Open Charge Point Protocol) is the industry-standard open protocol that connects charger hardware to management software. Sourcing **OCPP-certified hardware** means we are never locked into one supplier — any OCPP-compliant charger works with any OCPP-compliant backend.

## 💰 7. Business Models

### Model 1: Hardware Distributor / Reseller
- Import and sell Level 2 wall boxes to EV dealerships, businesses, property developers
- **Margin: 25–40%** per unit (market is new, premium pricing is acceptable)
- Low operational complexity — fastest to market

### Model 2: Charge Point Operator (CPO)
- Deploy chargers at owned or leased locations
- Revenue from electricity resale (BDT 11.36/kWh regulated + service charge markup)
- Target locations: shopping malls, corporate campuses, hotels, highway rest stops, residential towers
- Builds recurring long-term revenue

### Model 3: Integrated Hardware + Platform Solution
- Import and brand chargers under own label
- Deploy a local Charging Management System (CMS)
- Sell to businesses as a **complete managed charging solution**:
  - Hardware supply and installation
  - Monthly per-charger management fee (SaaS)
  - Usage analytics and reporting dashboard
  - Branded customer-facing app
- Highest value proposition — deepest competitive moat

### Model 4: Dealer OEM Partnership
- Partner with BYD Bangladesh, Deepal (DHS Autos), MG (Rangs Motors)
- Supply co-branded wall boxes bundled with vehicle purchase
- Dealers need a charging answer for their customers — we provide it
- Volume-based, brand association, low customer acquisition cost

## 📊 8. Financial Overview

### Unit Economics — Level 2 Wall Box (7kW)

```
China FOB price:              $150 – $250 per unit
Freight + customs clearance:  $30 – $50 per unit
Total landed cost:            ~$180 – $300 per unit

Local selling price:          BDT 45,000 – 75,000 (~$400 – $680)
Gross margin per unit:        35% – 45%
```

### Energy Revenue — CPO Model

```
Regulated electricity cost (BERC):  BDT 11.36 / kWh
Operator service charge (added):    BDT 3 – 8 / kWh
Total charged to EV driver:         BDT 14 – 20 / kWh
Margin on energy sold:              ~25% – 50%
```

## 📚 Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|:---|:---|
| **EVSE** | Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment — the technical name for a charging station |
| **OCPP** | Open Charge Point Protocol — the open industry standard connecting chargers to management software |
| **CMS / CPMS** | Charge Point Management System — backend software managing charging stations, billing, and users |
| **CPO** | Charge Point Operator — a company that owns and operates public charging stations |
| **Type 2** | The international AC charging connector standard — used by all Chinese EVs sold in Bangladesh |
| **CCS2** | Combined Charging System Type 2 — the DC fast charging standard for international-spec vehicles |
| **GB/T** | China's domestic EV charging standard — **incompatible with vehicles sold in Bangladesh** |
| **OCPP 1.6J / 2.0.1** | Versions of the OCPP protocol — 2.0.1 is the latest, with enhanced security and smart charging |
| **DLB** | Dynamic Load Balancing — technology that distributes available power across multiple chargers |
| **V2G** | Vehicle-to-Grid — future technology allowing EV batteries to supply power back to the grid |
| **BERC** | Bangladesh Energy Regulatory Commission — sets regulated electricity tariffs |
| **BRTA** | Bangladesh Road Transport Authority — EV registration and CPO licensing |
| **BIDA** | Bangladesh Investment Development Authority — investor facilitation and support |
| **NBR** | National Board of Revenue — customs and import duty authority |
| **HS 8504.40** | The harmonized tariff code for EV chargers (static converters) |
