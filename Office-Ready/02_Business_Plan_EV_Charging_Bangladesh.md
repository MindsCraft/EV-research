## 1. Executive Summary

Bangladesh stands at the beginning of an electric vehicle transition. The national government has set a target of EVs comprising 15–30% of all vehicles by 2030, eliminated all import duties on EV chargers in the FY 2026-27 budget, and reduced EV import taxes sharply to accelerate adoption. The formal passenger EV market is growing at 10–12% annually, with Runner Automobiles signing a BYD local assembly deal in early 2026 — signalling the start of serious market volume.

There are currently fewer than 20 functional public EV charging stations in Bangladesh serving a fast-growing fleet. The government has set a target of **1,200 stations** nationwide. This gap is our business opportunity.

We will establish a **full-service EV charging network** operating as a Charge Point Operator (CPO) and technology platform provider. We will import hardware from verified Chinese manufacturers, deploy chargers at homes, hotels, shopping centres, and city fast-charging locations, and run a proprietary software platform to manage billing, monitoring, and customer experience across the entire network.

**Our primary competitive advantage** is operational sophistication — bringing software-managed, data-driven charging infrastructure to a market where competitors have deployed basic hardware with minimal technology investment.

## 2. Market Opportunity

### Bangladesh EV Market — Key Facts (2026)

| Indicator | Data |
|:---|:---|
| Formal passenger EV registrations | ~1,000+ sold since 2024; growing 10–12% annually |
| Total EVs on road (incl. three-wheelers) | 25,000 – 30,000 units |
| Government EV target by 2030 | 15–30% of all vehicles |
| Public EV charging stations today | Fewer than 20 functional stations |
| Government target — public stations | 1,200 stations nationwide |
| Import duty on EV chargers (FY 2026-27) | 0% (previously 39.75%) |
| Key EV entrant — 2026 | Runner Automobiles × BYD (local assembly deal, March 2026) |

### Why Now Is the Right Time

1. **Zero import duty window** — the tax exemption on EV charger imports is new and creates immediate cost advantage for early movers before the market attracts larger competitors.
2. **Infrastructure deficit** — fewer than 20 stations serving a growing fleet creates genuine customer pain. Whoever builds reliable charging first earns loyalty and location lock-in.
3. **Competitor weakness** — existing operators (Ekhon Charge, Crack Platoon) are hardware deployers with basic apps. Neither has a software-managed network, proper billing integration, or remote diagnostics capability.
4. **Established local presence** — we enter with existing business relationships, local establishment, and credibility in Bangladesh — removing the market access barrier that would stop a new entrant.

### Lessons from Comparable Markets
*Research verified June 2026. Full detailed analysis available in the companion document: [03_Global_Market_Lessons.md](./03_Global_Market_Lessons.md)*

| Market | Stage in 2026 | Critical Lesson | Direct Action for Bangladesh |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| 🇮🇳 **India** | Reliability crisis — chargers exist but don't work | Uptime is the product, not the charger. 25%+ of India's chargers are non-functional. | Remote diagnostics + 24hr repair SLA on every unit from Day 1 |
| 🇻🇳 **Vietnam** | VinFast controls 150,000+ ports across all provinces | Infrastructure before mass adoption = market capture. Latecomers can't get locations. | Sign site agreements now — before Runner-BYD cars hit the road |
| 🇮🇩 **Indonesia** | Growth stalled when import incentives expired end-2025 | Policy tailwinds don't last. 0% import duty will change. | Build the model to work at 15–20% duty as a financial stress test |
| 🇨🇳 **China** | Margin war — volume without utilisation is a loss | Location quality over quantity. Brand hardware. Software is the real moat. | OEM white-label hardware + CPMS platform = defensible business |
| 🇳🇵 **Nepal** | 73% of new cars are EVs — only ~1,000 chargers exist | EV adoption can outpace infrastructure in under 2 years | Bangladesh faces the same gap — first-mover builds the market |
| 🇵🇰 **Pakistan** | Early market, best locations still unclaimed | Every month of delay = competitor gains an unchallenged foothold | Move on site agreements and supplier contracts before Q3 2026 |
| 🇱🇰 **Sri Lanka** | Regulation formalised in 2026 — informal operators being forced out | SREDA tightening is inevitable in Bangladesh too | SREDA-compliant from Day 1 — build it in, not on top |

**The 3 laws every market confirmed without exception:**
1. **Location lock-in** — the operator who secures premium sites first wins permanently
2. **Uptime wins** — drivers trust the network that works, not the one with the most chargers
3. **Software is the moat** — hardware commoditises; a proprietary platform does not

## 3. Our Business Model

> **Exchange Rate Reference (June 2026):** 1 USD = **BDT 122.85** (Bangladesh Bank / commercial rate)

We operate three interconnected revenue streams:

### Stream 1 — Hardware Sales & Installation
Import chargers from verified Chinese manufacturers at 0% duty, sell and install at client sites.

| Item | Unit Economics (USD) | Unit Economics (BDT) |
|:---|:---|:---|
| AC Wallbox (avg. cost $300 FOB + shipping) | Land cost: ~$350 | BDT ~43,000 |
| AC Wallbox sell price to client | ~$500 – $700 | BDT 61,000 – 86,000 |
| **Gross margin per AC unit** | **~$150 – $350** | **BDT ~18,000 – 43,000** |
| DC Fast Charger (avg. cost $4,000 FOB + shipping) | Land cost: ~$4,500 | BDT ~5,53,000 |
| DC Fast Charger sell price to client | ~$6,500 – $8,000 | BDT 7,99,000 – 9,83,000 |
| **Gross margin per DC unit** | **~$2,000 – $3,500** | **BDT ~2,46,000 – 4,30,000** |

Upsell: OEM white-label branded hardware (Autel Energy, Joint Tech) adds brand value and justifies 10–15% price premium.

---

### Stream 2 — Network Management SaaS Fee
Monthly recurring fee per charger connected to our platform. Covers: remote monitoring, OTA firmware, billing management, fault alerting, support.

| Charger Type | Monthly Fee (BDT) | Monthly Fee (USD) | Annual Per Charger (BDT) | Annual Per Charger (USD) |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| AC Wallbox (home/hotel) | BDT 1,000 – 1,500 | $8 – $12 | BDT 12,000 – 18,000 | $98 – $147 |
| AC Smart (mall/office) | BDT 1,500 – 2,000 | $12 – $16 | BDT 18,000 – 24,000 | $147 – $195 |
| DC Fast (public/hub) | BDT 2,000 – 3,000 | $16 – $24 | BDT 24,000 – 36,000 | $195 – $293 |

This is **pure recurring revenue** — it grows automatically with every charger deployed and requires no additional sales effort.

---

### Stream 3 — CPO Session Revenue
At locations we operate directly (public parking, city fast-charging hubs), we charge drivers per kWh and share revenue with the site host.

| Item | Rate |
|:---|:---|
| Charge to EV driver (per kWh) | BDT 25 – 35 / kWh (~$0.20 – $0.28) |
| Electricity purchase cost (DESCO/DPDC) | ~BDT 10 – 13 / kWh (~$0.08 – $0.11) |
| Gross margin per kWh | ~BDT 12 – 22 / kWh (~$0.10 – $0.18) |
| Platform commission (our share) | 20% of session revenue |
| Site host share | 80% of session revenue |
| Avg. session per DC charger per day | 8 – 15 sessions |
| Avg. kWh per session | 20 – 35 kWh |
| **Daily revenue per DC charger (our 20%)** | **BDT 800 – 3,700 / day** (**$6.50 – $30 / day**) |

## 4. Deployment Strategy — Phase 1

### Target Location Types

| Type | Charger | Why |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Private homes** | AC 7kW–22kW wallbox | Growing BYD/EV owner base needs overnight charging at home |
| **Apartment buildings** | AC 7kW–22kW (multi-unit) | Shared parking + managed billing per resident via app |
| **Hotels (3–5 star)** | AC 11kW–22kW | EV guests as premium segment; amenity differentiator for hotel |
| **Shopping centres** | DC 30–60kW + AC 22kW | High dwell time (2–3 hrs) suits DC fast charging revenue model |
| **Office buildings & campuses** | AC 22kW | Employees charge during work hours — predictable demand |
| **City public parking** | DC 50–60kW | High turnover; revenue-maximising locations |
| **City fast charging hubs** | DC 60–120kW | Strategic locations in Dhaka, Chattogram, Sylhet, Rajshahi |

### Priority City Locations — Dhaka
Gulshan-1 & 2, Banani, Baridhara, Dhanmondi, Bashundhara, Uttara, Motijheel CBD

### Phase 1 — Out of Scope
Large vehicle charging, bus depot charging, and highway corridor charging. These require different infrastructure investment and grid sanctions not yet ready in Bangladesh's passenger EV growth stage.

|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Home / Hotel | Joint Tech | AC 7–22kW Wallbox | $150 – $450 |
| Mall / Office | EVB (Beny) | AC + DC 22kW / 60kW | $200 – $9,000 |
| Mall / Public Parking | Iocharger | DC 30–60kW (DLB) | $2,650 – $5,500 |
| City Hub / Premium | Autel Energy | AC + DC 50–120kW | $280 – $22,000 |
| Grid-Limited City Hub | XCharge GridLink | DC 60–300kW (battery-buffered) | $5,500 – $60,000 |
| High-Volume DC | StarCharge | DC 30–360kW | $3,000 – $35,000 |
| Luxury / Solar Home | Sigenergy | V2H 12.5–25kW bidirectional | $4,000 – $8,500 |

**Key hardware principles:**
- All units OCPP 1.6J or 2.0.1 compliant — works with any software platform
- No GB/T connectors — only Type 2 (AC) and CCS2 (DC) for Bangladesh EV compatibility
- Built-in surge protection (Class I+II SPD) mandatory — Bangladesh monsoon lightning risk
- IP54 or higher rated for outdoor humidity and heat exposure

## 6. Software Platform

**Status:** To be built or licensed. This is the next major business decision after board approval of the hardware strategy.

### What the Platform Must Do

| Function | Description |
|:---|:---|
| **OCPP Gateway** | Connect and manage all chargers via OCPP 1.6J / 2.0.1 WebSocket protocol |
| **Driver Mobile App** | iOS & Android — map, QR scan, real-time session data, bKash/Nagad/card payment |
| **Guest Web Flow** | QR → mobile web → pay → session starts. No app download required. |
| **Host Portal** | Site owner dashboard — tariff setting, revenue tracking, automated payout |
| **Operator Console** | Internal dashboard — live network map, remote control, OTA firmware, load management |
| **Billing Engine** | Per-kWh, per-minute, flat session, Time-of-Use, idle fee billing models |
| **Payment Settlement** | Automated weekly payouts to hosts via BEFTN bank transfer or bKash/Nagad merchant API |

### Build vs. Buy Options

| Option | Description | Cost | Timeline |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Open-source base** (CitrineOS / SteVe) | Self-hosted OCPP server; build UI on top | Low licence cost; high dev effort | 4–8 months |
| **White-label CPMS** (Ampeco / Virta) | Licenced platform, branded as ours | $1,000–3,000/month licence | 4–8 weeks to deploy |
| **Custom build from scratch** | Full ownership; maximum flexibility | Highest upfront cost | 8–18 months |

> **Recommendation:** White-label a proven platform for Year 1 launch speed, then progressively migrate to custom-built as revenue justifies the development investment.

## 10. Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| EV adoption slower than projected | Medium | High | Phase 1 capital is modest; hardware can be redeployed; start with pre-committed sites |
| Grid capacity limits at commercial sites | High | Medium | Use DLB-capable chargers (Iocharger, EVB); pre-survey every site before installation |

| Software platform delay | Medium | High | Use white-label platform for launch; do not wait for custom build |
| Currency fluctuation (BDT/USD) | Medium | Medium | Negotiate FOB contracts with fixed price; maintain USD reserves for imports |
| Regulatory changes | Low | Medium | Engage SREDA early; maintain compliance as a priority; track NBR announcements |
| Competition from Indian/global CPOs entering BD | Low (Year 1–2) | High | Establish location lock-in agreements with premium sites before competition arrives |
